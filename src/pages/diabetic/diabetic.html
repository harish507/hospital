Diabetics

Diabetic Emergency

Introduction

Diabetes is a lifelong medical condition where the body cannot produce enough insulin. Insulin is a chemical made by the pancreas (a gland behind the stomach), which regulates the blood sugar (glucose) level in the body.

What to look for - Diabetic emergency

High blood sugar (hyperglycaemia)
  1. Warm, dry skin

  2. Rapid pulse and breathing

  3. Fruity sweet breath

  4. Really thirsty

  5. Drowsiness, leading to unresponsiveness if not treated
Low blood sugar (hypoglycaemia)
  1. Weakness, faintness or hunger
  2. Confusion and irrational behaviour
  3. Sweating with cold, clammy skin
  4. Rapid pulse
  5. Trembling
  6. Deteriorating level of response
  7. Medical warning bracelet or necklace and glucose gel or sweets
  8. Medication such as an insulin pen or tablets and a glucose testing kit
