Abs Worksouts

Abdominal Crunches

Click Here  to know more.

Lie on your back with your knees bent and your arms stretched forward. Then lift your upper body off the floor. Hold for a few seconds and slowly return. It primarily works the rectus abdominis muscle and the obliques.

Russian Twist

Click Here  to know more.

Sit on the floor with your knees bent, feet lifted a little bit and back tilted backwards. Then hold your hands together and twist from side to side.

Mountain Climber

Click Here  to know more.

Start in the push-up position. Bend your right knee towards your chest and keep your left leg straight, then quickly switch from one leg to the other. This exercise strengthens multiple muscle groups.

Heel Touch

Click Here  to know more.

Lie on the ground with your legs bent and your arms by your sides. Slightly lift your upper body off the floor and make your hands alternately reach your heels.

Leg Raises

Click Here  to know more.

Lie down on your back, and put your hands beneath your hips for support. Then lift your legs up until they form a right angle with the floor. Slowly bring your legs back down and repeat the exercise.

Plank

Click Here  to know more.

Lie on the floor with your toes and forearms on the ground. Keep your body straight and hold this position as long as you can. This exercise strengthens the abdomen, back and shoulders.

Spine Lumber Twist Stretch

Click Here  to know more.

Lie on your back with your legs extended. Lift your left leg up and use your right hand to pull your left knee to the right, but keep your other arm extended to the side on the floor. Hold this position for a few seconds.

Superman

Click Here  to know more.

Put your spine into the neutral position. Activate your core by drawing your abdominal muscles back toward your spine. Without any sideways movement and keeping your abdomen pulled in, exhale and slowly raise an opposite arm and until they are parallel to the floor be careful to follow the tempo you have set.

Rotating T Extension

Click Here  to know more.

Start out in the classic pushup motion, but as you are pushing yourself upward, rotate your body so your right arm lifts up and extends overhead, as illustrated. Your arms and torso should form a T. Return to the starting position, lower ourself, the push up and rotate until our left hand points toward the ceiling.

Bodyweight Hip Extension

Click Here  to know more.

Lie flat on the floor or mat with one leg extended straight and the other leg bent with the foot flat on the floor. Place your arms down on the mat, on the side of your hips. Raise your lower back off the floor while keeping your extended leg straight. Hold, then return to your original position After a few repetitions, switch legs and repeat with the other side.

Side Bridge

Click Here  to know more.

Lie on your side. Make sure one leg is on top of the other. Position the forearm on the floor perpendicular with your body. Use your forearm to lift your upper body of the ground. The other arm is placed on the side of your body. Your body should be straight. Only your forearm and the side of your foot should be touching the floor. Hold this position.

Twisting Hip Raise

Click Here  to know more.

Lie down on the floor. Wrap weight around upper body with your arms. Keep knees bent and feet flat on floor. Raise your upper body upward while keeping your lower back on the floor. Hold for one second. Return to starting position.

Dumbell Side Bends

Click Here  to know more.

Stand straight with feet shoulder width apart. Hold a dumbbell in each hand. Bend downwards in one direction, with minimum movement to your lower body. Return to starting position.
